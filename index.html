The core thesis is straightforward: if quantum risk becomes operational before Bitcoin is fully adapted, the ecosystem will need credible protection infrastructure in the interim.

Mission

We are building the protective layer for the period in which Bitcoin may be most exposed.

Protect holders

Provide Bitcoin holders with a credible framework for navigating a high-impact security transition instead of relying on denial, panic, or last-minute migration.

Protect market structure

Reduce the chance that quantum fear triggers disorderly exits, cascading liquidations, or avoidable trust failures across the broader Bitcoin ecosystem.

Create time for the network

Help create a survivable bridge between today’s security assumptions and tomorrow’s quantum-safe Bitcoin architecture.

Protection Model

A three-part operating model for managing the quantum transition.

01

Detect emerging threat conditions

Monitor the conditions that matter most: wallet exposure, migration readiness, market stress, and the thresholds at which latent risk becomes actionable.

02

Provide holder-side safeguards

Design practical mechanisms that allow serious Bitcoin participants to defend value before a full network-level solution is broadly available.

03

Accelerate network resilience

Support the path toward quantum-safe standards, migration tooling, and coordinated adoption so temporary protection can eventually become unnecessary.

Why This Matters

Bitcoin is strongest when conviction is matched by preparation.

Long-term holders have spent years preparing for volatility, regulation, and custody risk. Quantum-era security pressure is different: it is technical, asymmetric, and easy to ignore until the window to react gets uncomfortably small.

Not all BTC is equally exposed

Older wallet patterns and public-key exposure dynamics create uneven risk across the network, which means preparedness cannot stay abstract.

Markets react faster than governance

Even limited evidence of a quantum-capable threat could change market behavior long before a network-wide response is implemented and adopted.

The bridge years matter

The most dangerous period may be the transition itself, when risk is visible but the final defense layer is still being rolled out.

Roadmap

From immediate safeguards to a quantum-secure end state.

Phase A

Research and threat framing

Establish a clear view of holder exposure, threat thresholds, and the actions that should take place before panic begins to set the agenda.

Phase B

Prototype protection systems

Build and test holder-side protection mechanisms that are legible, credible, and usable by serious Bitcoin participants.

Phase C

Coordinate migration readiness

Support the tools, communications, and ecosystem alignment needed to move from temporary defense into durable network-level protection.

FAQ

A serious mission, explained clearly.

Is this anti-Bitcoin?

No. The premise is the opposite: Bitcoin is worth protecting early, carefully, and credibly before a known class of future threats turns into a forced reaction.

Why a foundation?

A foundation structure fits the mission: research, ecosystem coordination, and protection infrastructure that is intended to serve Bitcoin holders and the broader network over the long term.

What does “NPUT” mean?

NPUT refers to the Nakamoto Input. The name is intended to signal a direct connection to Bitcoin’s origin while giving the foundation a concise, distinct public identity.
