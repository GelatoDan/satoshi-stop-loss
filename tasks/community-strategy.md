# Task: Community & Developer Relations Strategy

## Objective
Design a detailed community and developer relations strategy for SSL. This is a focused complement to the full marketing plan — it goes deeper on the human/relationship layer that determines whether a DeFi protocol builds a lasting community or a ghost town.

## Output
Save to `working/ssl-community-devrel-strategy-[date].docx`

## Section 1: Community Architecture

### Platform Strategy
Research and recommend the community platform mix. Do not just recommend everything — make hard choices about where to concentrate energy. Consider:
- **Discord:** Standard for DeFi. What server structure works for a protocol with two distinct user types (vault users vs. IF stakers)? Design the channel architecture.
- **Telegram:** Still large in crypto, especially for price/news. What role, if any?
- **X/Twitter:** The crypto native town square. What is SSL's Twitter persona and content cadence?
- **Farcaster:** Growing crypto-native social. Worth early presence?
- **Lens:** Decentralized social. Worth it pre-launch?
- **Reddit:** r/DeFi, r/Bitcoin, r/CryptoCurrency. Organic vs. managed presence?
- **Governance forum:** Commonwealth or Discourse for on-chain governance discussions. When to launch?

For each platform: recommended role, content type, posting cadence, who manages it.

### Community Segments Within the Protocol
SSL has fundamentally different community segments with different interests. Design how the community structure accommodates both without conflict:
- **Vault users:** Want protection, reliability, clear communication about protocol health
- **IF stakers:** Want yield information, risk transparency, governance influence
- **Developers / integrators:** Want technical docs, support, integration grants
- **Governance participants:** Want proposal discussion, voting history, delegate infrastructure
- **Traders / speculators:** Will be in the community whether you want them or not — how do you manage this segment?

## Section 2: Ambassador & Moderator Program

Design a formal ambassador program:
- **Roles:** Core team → Moderators → Ambassadors → Community members. Define each.
- **Ambassador requirements:** What does someone need to demonstrate to become an ambassador?
- **Ambassador benefits:** Token allocation? Early access? Exclusive channels? Co-authorship of research?
- **Geographic diversity:** Research where the largest crypto communities are globally and design for geographic representation
- **Compensation model:** Paid vs. volunteer vs. token-incentivized. Research what has worked for comparable protocols.

## Section 3: Developer Relations

SSL needs developers in two ways: (1) auditors and security researchers who will stress-test the protocol, (2) integration developers who will build on top of SSL or integrate it into their protocols.

### Security Research Community
- Bug bounty program design: scope, reward tiers, disclosure policy
- How to attract top security researchers? Research: Immunefi, Code4rena, Sherlock — which has the best community for a protocol like SSL?
- Relationship with audit firms: Trail of Bits, Spearbit, Cantina — how do you build ongoing relationships vs. one-time audits?

### Integration Developer Community
- SDK and API design for developers who want to integrate SSL into their protocols
- Developer documentation strategy (see also marketing plan)
- Hackathon strategy: should SSL sponsor hackathons? Which ones? (ETHGlobal, Solana Breakpoint, Chainlink hackathons)
- Developer grants program: how much to allocate, what types of projects, selection process

## Section 4: Governance Community Design

SSL governance starts centralized (founding team) and decentralizes over time. Design the governance community:
- **Progressive decentralization roadmap:** What decisions does the team retain control of? What gets handed to governance, and when?
- **Delegate infrastructure:** Should SSL have a formal delegate program (like Uniswap, Optimism)? Who are the ideal early delegates? Research: crypto governance delegates, DeFi security researchers, protocol operators who participate in other governance systems.
- **Governance engagement tactics:** Low voter participation is endemic in DeFi. Research what's worked to improve governance participation. Design specific mechanics for SSL.
- **Community calls:** Cadence, format, what gets discussed

## Section 5: Content Calendar Framework

Design a 12-month content calendar framework (not specific posts, but recurring content types and cadence):
- Weekly: What goes out every week without fail?
- Monthly: What recurring content builds authority over time?
- Event-triggered: What content fires when BTC moves, when governance proposals are live, when new chains launch?
- Crisis: What does the comms cadence look like during a governance dispute, a security incident, or a false positive trigger?

## Section 6: Metrics for Community Health

Define what a healthy SSL community looks like vs. an unhealthy one:
- Discord: active users, message volume, % channels active, moderator response time
- Governance: voter participation rate, proposal quality, delegate diversity
- Developer: integrations live, GitHub activity (public repos), hackathon submissions
- Sentiment: how to monitor and respond to community sentiment shifts

What are the early warning signs that community health is deteriorating? Design a monitoring system.
