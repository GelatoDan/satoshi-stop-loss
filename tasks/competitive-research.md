# Task: Competitive Landscape Research

## Objective
Produce a comprehensive competitive landscape analysis for SSL. This should be deep enough to inform positioning, pitch deck, and marketing messaging.

## Output
Save to `research/competitive-landscape-[date].docx`

## Research Scope

### Direct Competitors (protocols addressing the same risk)
Search for any protocols that:
- Offer crypto portfolio insurance or stop-loss mechanisms
- Monitor on-chain wallet activity and trigger automated actions
- Provide DeFi insurance against specific on-chain events

Key protocols to research: Nexus Mutual, InsurAce, Risk Harbor, UnoRe, Neptune Mutual, Sherlock, Ease.org (formerly Armor), Unslashed Finance. Look for any others that have launched since early 2025.

For each, document: product description, TVL, token market cap, fee model, what risks they cover, what they don't cover, key weaknesses.

### Indirect Competitors (different mechanism, overlapping user job-to-be-done)
- Centralized exchange stop-loss orders (Coinbase Advanced, Binance, Kraken)
- Options protocols: Lyra, Dopex, Hegic, Premia — can users hedge Satoshi risk via puts?
- Prediction markets: Polymarket, Manifold — is there a Satoshi spend market?
- Structured products: Ribbon Finance, Friktion (Solana) — any BTC downside products?

### Adjacent Infrastructure
- Bitcoin oracle/monitoring services: who currently watches Satoshi wallets professionally?
- Cross-chain bridge security: any protocols monitoring for large BTC movements?
- MEV/liquidation infrastructure: who are the sophisticated liquidators in DeFi today?

## Analysis Framework
For each competitor, produce a structured entry:

**[Protocol Name]**
- Category: Direct / Indirect / Adjacent
- What it does (2–3 sentences)
- TVL / AUM (current, search for latest)
- Token market cap (current)
- Fee model
- What risk it covers
- Key strength
- Key weakness
- SSL differentiation vs. this protocol

## Synthesis Section
After the individual entries, write:

1. **Competitive moat analysis:** What makes SSL defensible? Where is it vulnerable?
2. **Positioning statement:** Where does SSL sit in the landscape? What is the one-sentence positioning?
3. **Key messaging implications:** What do competitors' weaknesses tell us about what SSL should emphasize?
4. **White space:** What user needs exist that no current protocol addresses well?

## Important Notes
- Use real, current data — search for it, don't estimate
- Flag any protocol that has shut down, been hacked, or been abandoned since its peak
- Note any protocols that attempted something similar to SSL and failed — and why
- The Satoshi wallet risk specifically is likely un-addressed by any existing protocol; confirm this
